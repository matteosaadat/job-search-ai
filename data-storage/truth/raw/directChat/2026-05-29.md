## Location

I live in Hardwick, VT. It's a rural town. The closest tech city is Burlington, VT — approximately 90 minutes from Hardwick. Montpelier is another nearby city, approximately 45 minutes away.

I don't mind hybrid work up to 1.5 hours away from Hardwick. Burlington and Montpelier are both within that range and are fine for hybrid roles.

There is a 90% chance that companies saying "onsite" will agree to remote or hybrid when they see the candidate is local. In software engineering, "onsite" very often just means "live nearby" — not literally in-office every day. This should be factored into how onsite jobs are scored.

I prefer remote work first. I will not relocate. I am willing to visit Boston occasionally because my sister lives there and I can stay with her. Short-term stays are fine. Monthly visits to Boston are acceptable for an otherwise remote role.

## Contract preferences

I prefer contract work over being employed full-time. Contract is my first choice (100%). Being employed is acceptable but less preferred (80%).

I prefer 3 days per week over 5 days per week. 3 days/week is ideal (100%). 5 days/week is acceptable but less preferred (80%).

These two dimensions are independent and multiply together:
- Contract, 3 days/week: 100% × 100% = 100%
- Contract, 5 days/week: 100% × 80% = 80%
- Part-time employed, 3 days/week: 80% × 100% = 80%
- Full-time employed, 5 days/week: 80% × 80% = 64%

For contract roles, preferred duration is 3 to 6 months. Minimum 2 months. Maximum 12 months.

## Rate

A work year is 2000 hours, not 2080. This accounts for 2 weeks vacation — 50 working weeks × 40 hours.

Full-time employed jobs come with benefits (401k, health insurance, etc.) worth approximately 30% on top of the base salary. When comparing a full-time salary to a contract hourly rate, multiply the salary-derived hourly by 1.30 to get total compensation equivalent. For example: $200k salary ÷ 2000 hours = $100/hr base × 1.30 = $130/hr total comp equivalent.

Minimum acceptable contract rate: $85/hour. Preferred contract rate: $120/hour. These thresholds apply to contract hourly rates. For full-time roles, apply the 30% benefits premium before comparing.

## Truth architecture

The truth system should work like this: raw human documents (messy, noisy, mixed) go into truth/raw/{source}/. These get atomized into domain-classified atoms in truth/atoms/{domain}/. Documents like rules.yaml and truth.md are generated from atoms — they are never edited directly. When a new atom is added, all documents that subscribe to that domain get re-synthesized. This is a broadcast/subscribe model. One new truth chip can cascade to change many derived rules.
